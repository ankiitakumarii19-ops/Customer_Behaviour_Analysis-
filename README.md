# Customer_Behaviour_Analysis-

Project: 
Fashion & Apparel Consumer Intelligence — Retail Behavioral Analytics

Overview:
This project decodes the purchasing DNA of 3,900 retail customers to transform a fashion brand’s marketing from "broad-cast" to "precision-targeted."By integrating demographic, seasonal, and loyalty data, the analysis provides a strategic roadmap to maximize **Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)** and bridge the 73% subscription penetration gap.

---

Executive Summary:
The business currently operates with a "Customer Intelligence Gap," lacking a structured framework to differentiate high-value segments from casual shoppers. This project delivers a multi-tool analytical pipeline—from Python-based segmentation to Power BI geospatial mapping—to optimize promotional ROI and align inventory with seasonal demand.

---

Key Customer Insights:

Customer Base: 3,900 unique shoppers across all 50 U.S. states.

Revenue Profile: Average purchase of $59.76 with transactions ranging from $20 to $100.

Gender Split: 68% Male / 32% Female.

Loyalty: 27% active subscribers with a mean of 25.4 previous purchases per customer.

Satisfaction: Average review rating of 3.75/5.0.

---

Strategic Objectives:

Behavioral Segmentation: Categorize shoppers into 7 distinct frequency tiers (Weekly to Annually) to identify the "MVP" segments.

Subscription Lift: Quantify the spending delta between subscribers and non-subscribers to drive acquisition strategies.

Seasonal Inventory Mapping:Align the 4 core categories (**Clothing, Footwear, Outerwear, Accessories**) with high-demand seasons.

Promo ROI: Evaluate if discount codes (used in 43% of orders) are driving incremental value or eroding margins.


Technical Workflow:
* Excel → Data Understanding & Cleaning
* Python → Data Validation & EDA & Feature Engineering
* SQL → KPI Analysis
* Power BI → Visualization & Dashboard

Project Constraints & Risks:

Time-Series Limit: Analysis relies on the "Season" column as no transaction dates were recorded.

Gender Bias: High male-to-female ratio (2.1:1) requires careful statistical weighting for female-specific insights.

Data Integrity: Review rating analysis excludes a minor 0.9% null rate (37 records).
